Extrapolating by Inverse Optimization

Wherein I present a simple example of a prediction problem modeled as inverse optimization, with a small detour into Bayesian estimation, peppered with snide remarks about data science as it is commonly practiced. Let us imagine a grocer trying to esimate the impacts of changing the pricing of her produce on the purchase behavior of her customers. Following the advice of her data scientist friend she gathers her database of quantities of produce purchased by individual customers under various pricing regimes, and hands him the trove.
